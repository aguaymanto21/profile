Improvement of Irrigation Water Supply and Canals in Tiabaya

Tiabaya Distric, Province of Arequipa - Arequipa

Executive Summary

Project Overview

Project Title: Improvement of Irrigation Water Supply in the Tiabaya Sub-sector, Arequipa.

Objective: Enhance irrigation services by rehabilitating five main channels using reinforced concrete, improving water distribution efficiency for local agriculture.

Overview: This project aims to upgrade irrigation infrastructure in Tiabaya to address inadequate water supply due to deteriorating channels. Managed by the Municipality of Tiabaya, it includes representatives Katia Valeria Silva Cisneros (Formulating Unit) and Efrain Charres Mestas (Executing Unit).

Location and Impact

Demographic Information

The Tiabaya district has experienced a steady population increase, with a current total of approximately 3,094 people, primarily engaged in agriculture. The population has grown due to activities in mining and tourism. The area retains a semi-urban characteristic, with most residents involved in vegetable and fruit cultivation. Tiabaya is an important producer of garlic, celery, and other vegetables in southern Peru, with products oriented toward agro-industrial and export markets.

Project Problem Statement

The current irrigation system in Tiabaya is insufficient, with poor infrastructure across five primary channels: the Principal, Agujerito, Los Pinos, Peral 3, and Corvacho channels. These channels are deteriorated, partially obstructed by vegetation, and lack efficient water management. The water supply varies seasonally from 1.09 MMC (February) to 1.21 MMC (other months), creating a need for effective infrastructure to ensure a steady supply for agriculture.
